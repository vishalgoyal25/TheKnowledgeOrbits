The Constitution of India is the supreme law, a comprehensive written document establishing India as a sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic with a parliamentary government, defining fundamental rights, guiding principles, and duties for citizens and institutions. Adopted on November 26, 1949, and enacted on January 26, 1950 (Republic Day), it's the world's longest national constitution, outlining India's federal structure, separation of powers (executive, legislature, judiciary), and ensuring rule of law, making it the highest legal authority.
Key Aspects:
Supreme Law: The foundational law of India, above all other laws and government bodies.
Enactment: Came into effect on January 26, 1950, after being adopted by the Constituent Assembly.
Framework: Defines the structure, powers, and functions of government, fundamental rights, and citizen duties.
Republic: Establishes India as a republic with an elected head of state (President).
Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic: Defines India's core principles.
Federal Structure: Balances power between the Union (central) and State governments.
Parliamentary System: Features an executive (Prime Minister-led cabinet) responsible to the legislature (Parliament).
Judiciary: An independent judiciary (Supreme Court, High Courts) interprets the Constitution and protects rights.
Key Features:
Longest Written Constitution: The most extensive national constitution globally, containing numerous articles, parts, and schedules.
Fundamental Rights & Duties: Guarantees essential rights (like freedom of speech, equality) and outlines citizen duties.
Directive Principles: Guides for the State in formulating policy.
Amendments: Has been amended over 100 times, with the Supreme Court safeguarding its "basic structure".
